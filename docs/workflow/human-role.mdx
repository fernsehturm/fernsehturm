# Human Role

Humans are not the primary implementers in Fernsehturm—agents are. The human role is focused on **intent, approval, and conflict resolution**.

## Approve decisions

Decisions shape the system in ways that are hard to reverse: technology choices, architectural patterns, API contracts. Humans review and approve (or reject) proposed decisions before they become active.

This is intentional. Agents are good at deriving and implementing. Humans are better at judging whether a decision is right for the broader context.

## Resolve conflicts

When the consistency engine finds conflicting artifacts—two requirements that cannot both be satisfied, two decisions that contradict each other—a human resolves the conflict by:

- Revising one of the conflicting artifacts
- Superseding an outdated artifact
- Creating a new decision that resolves the ambiguity

## Refine intent

Initial goals are often underspecified. As requirements are derived, gaps and ambiguities surface. Humans refine the goal or add clarifying requirements to steer the agent in the right direction.

## What humans do NOT do

- Edit files directly (the workspace is disposable)
- Review every line of generated code (the consistency engine does that)
- Write boilerplate (agents handle implementation)

The human role is strategic, not tactical.
